Overview

SailWindow V6 — Gulf Coast Edition. Fetches live hourly wind forecasts from NWS and tide predictions from NOAA Tides & Currents, scores every possible time block against your preferences, and shows the best sailing window for today. Covers 35 verified NOAA stations from Port Isabel, TX to Key West, FL. No login required — preferences are saved in your browser.

Sailability Score

The large number (0–10) rates conditions during the best available window. The colour badge describes it in plain English:

🟢 Excellent9–10 · Prime conditions, go sailing
✦ Good7–8.9 · Solid day, comfortably in range
🟡 Marginal5–6.9 · Sail with awareness
🔴 No-Go0–4.9 · Stay ashore

The reason tags below the score summarise wind, gusts, rain, storm risk, and wind direction during the window.

Best Sailing Window

SailWindow scans all daylight blocks (7 AM – 6 PM) matching your Trip Duration, scores each, and shows the best departure and return time. A green shaded band on both the Wind and Tide charts marks the same window visually.

Conditions Panel

Four tiles show key metrics measured during the best window:

💨 Wind
Speed range (kt) + direction. Ideal = inside your preferred range.
⚡ Gusts
Peak gust vs. your limit. Amber = near limit. Red = exceeds limit.
🌊 Tide
Favorable = tide direction matches your preference (or you chose Any).
⛈ Storm Risk
Average rain probability. Red if thunderstorms appear in the forecast.
Wind & Tide Charts

Wind chart — 24-hour hourly view. Teal = sustained wind (kt), amber dashed = gusts, green band = best window. Tap to read exact values for any hour.

Tide chart — full-day tide curve in feet MLLW, best window shaded. The Tide Events table below it lists each high/low with local time and height.

7-Day Forecast

Tap 7-Day in the bottom nav to see a full week of sailability scores and best windows, powered by the ECMWF IFS model via Open-Meteo — a high-resolution European forecast model well-suited for Gulf Coast marine conditions.

Sailing Preferences

Open the settings sheet to adjust:

Wind Min / Max (kt) — ideal sustained range. Hours outside it are penalised.
Max Gust (kt) — any hour above this scores 0 (automatic no-go).
Duration (hrs) — length of your sail (0.5–12 hrs). Sets the window width searched.
Tide Preference — Any, Incoming (flood), or Outgoing (ebb). Matching hours get a small scoring bonus.

Tap Save & Refresh to apply and reload live data immediately.

Location & Marina Map

Tap Change on the location bar to use device GPS, pick from 60 Gulf & Atlantic preset stations (Texas → Louisiana → Mississippi → Alabama → Florida), or open the Marina Map. For unlisted locations use Preferences → Custom GPS / NOAA Station.

The Marina tab shows a searchable list and interactive map. On phones, tap the 🗺 icon to toggle between the list and the map. Marina data is cached for 24 hours — the map loads instantly on return visits.

Navigation Advisory

The amber advisory banner at the top of the Marina list covers hurricane-damaged facilities — Panama City, Clearwater, Fort Myers, and others — that are technically listed as open but have suspended transient docking or fueling. Tap the banner to expand the full status by storm. Marina cards for affected facilities show an ⚠ Advisory badge.

"Open" can be misleading. Always confirm transient availability by phone before arriving.

Feedback

Tap Feedback in the bottom nav to report a marina that has reopened, newly closed, has wrong contact info, or to share app ideas. Select a category, add details, and tap Send — your email app opens with the report pre-filled.

How the Score is Calculated

Each hour starts at 10. Hard disqualifiers drop it to 0: wind below 4 kt or above 26 kt, gusts over your max, rain ≥ 70%, or thunderstorms in the forecast. Otherwise points are deducted for wind outside your preferred range, gusty spread, rain probability, and mismatched tide direction. The best block is selected using a 70/30 blend of average and minimum score, so one dangerous hour can't hide inside an otherwise good window.

Troubleshooting
Data won't load
Check your internet connection. NWS occasionally returns 500 errors — wait a minute and tap Refresh Live Data.
Score seems too low
Check your wind range — a narrow range penalises most days. Also verify the correct location is active.
GPS not working
Allow location access in your browser settings. GPS auto-selects the nearest of the 32 built-in NOAA stations — full Gulf Coast coverage from Brownsville, TX to Key West, FL.

Marinas

If you are navigating the Intracoastal or the Gulf Coast, "open" can be a deceptive word. Many marinas across Southwest Florida and the Tampa Bay region are technically operational for local slip holders but have suspended all transient docking, high-capacity fueling, or shore-power hookups due to ongoing grid and dock maintenance.

Panhandle — Hurricane Michael

The vast majority of Panhandle marinas successfully rebuilt or upgraded after Michael. One prominent public facility remains the exception:

  • Panama City Downtown Marina ⚠ — Still non-operational for transient and slip-holder use. Hurricane Michael delivered catastrophic damage to seawalls, docks, utilities, and upland buildings — including the permanent loss of its ship's store. Rather than a patch repair, the City has launched a full waterfront revitalization: public design charrettes are complete, a multi-million dollar master plan for modernized slips, expanded boardwalks, and new dining is approved, but full vertical construction remains in progress.
  • St. Andrews Marina (Panama City) ✓ Reopened — Welcoming transient boaters again under new management (Coastal Marina Management, April 2026). Fuel dock pumping; bulkhead reconstruction adding 44 slips approved and underway, with plans for 150+. Harbormaster: (850) 872-7240.
  • Tyndall AFB Marina ✓ Rebuilt — Completely destroyed by Michael, then rebuilt as a brand-new 42,000+ sq ft marina complex (restaurant, boat slips, recreational storage) as part of the $4.7B base-wide reconstruction. Targeted completion: early 2025.
  • Mexico Beach Marina ✓ Open — Took the direct Category 5 eyewall hit but was successfully rebuilt as a private commercial facility. Fully open with operational fuel docks, ship's store, and regular hours.

West Coast & Tampa Bay — Helene & Milton

  • Clearwater Beach Marina ⚠ — Fuel dock permanently closed. Full facility reconstruction underway through late summer 2026; Harbormaster's office destroyed, staff operating off-site.
  • Dunedin Marina ⚠ — Heavy structural reconstruction through mid-to-late 2026; public boat ramp closed to general public.
  • Safety Harbor Marina & Pier ⚠ — Entire basin, boat ramp, and pier closed through December 2026. Complete reconstruction contract awarded to Shoreline Foundation.

Southwest Florida — Hurricane Ian

  • Fort Myers / Fort Myers Beach ⚠ — Post-Ian recovery ongoing. Large corporate-backed marinas (Safe Harbor properties) have rebuilt and expanded; smaller operators may still have compromised seawalls or remain closed. Call ahead before arriving for transient availability.

Always confirm transient availability by phone before arriving. Conditions change frequently.
