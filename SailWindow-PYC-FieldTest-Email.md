**Subject:** Invite for PYC: free field-test of a Pensacola Bay sailing-conditions tool

Hi Cesar,

I'm Howard, a Gulf Coast sailor and the builder of SailWindow (sailwindow.com). I'm reaching out to a handful of Pensacola clubs to find a small group of real sailors to field-test it — and Pensacola Yacht Club is at the top of my list.

Here's the short version of what it does: SailWindow reads the NOAA Pensacola and Pensacola Beach Pier stations, scores the day 0–10 against *your own* wind and gust comfort range, and tells you the best departure window with the tides built in. It's the "is today worth it, for me, on my water" decision — not a passage planner, not offshore routing. It sells to the morning, not to the trip.

**Why I'd love PYC to test it:**

- **Pensacola is already a free region.** Both local stations are on the free tier, so every member can use SailWindow today at zero cost — there's nothing to buy to try it.
- **It scores the day for *you*, not in general.** Set your wind and gust comfort range once and the 0–10 sailability score reflects your boat and your tolerance, not a generic forecast.
- **Best departure window, tides included.** It points to the part of the day actually worth going out, with tide predictions baked in.
- **Real data, no fuss.** Live hourly wind from NWS, tide predictions from NOAA, and a 7-day outlook. Runs in any browser — no app store, no login, and your preferences stay on your own device.
- **Built for bay sailors.** Everything in it serves the daily go/no-go call of a local sailor on local water.

**The freebie part:** for anyone at PYC willing to kick the tires and send me honest feedback, I'll issue a free-forever code so the full premium version costs them nothing during the test and after. I'm happy to set up your test crew with codes individually, and I can give the club a member discount code (20% off, forever) for any broader rollout down the road.

All I'm hoping for in return is candid reactions — what's useful, what's confusing, what's missing. Your sailors know Pensacola Bay far better than any forecast does, and that's exactly the feedback that makes this better.

Could I send you a one-page intro to drop in front of a few members, or grab 10 minutes to show it live at an upcoming meeting? Either works — whatever's easiest on your end.
